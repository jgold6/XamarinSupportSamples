Deceptively simple, amazingly powerful

Customer data is stored on the RFID tag embedded in coupling insert. Customer has the option to decide if data should be modifiable or write-protected.

RFID tag can be programmed with:

  • Product identifiers
  • Lot codes
  • Batch information
  • Calibration data
The coupling body has an RFID reader that "looks for" the tag.
The two coupling halves connect.
  • Once the two halves are a few centimeters apart, the reader detects the tag, reads it and sends the tag data to the main controller
  • The reader can also write new information to the tag
The equipment acts according to the coupling insert data sent to the controller. The reader can write new data to the tag.
Customers are now able to transfer more than just fluids through the point of connection.

IdentiQuik products can be used to store and transfer information such as:

  • Product identification
  • Batch information (e.g., date of manufacture)
  • Date code
  • Cycle counts
  • Auto-calibration
  • Notification of cycle completion
  • Media parameter verification
  • Weight
  • Product characteristics
